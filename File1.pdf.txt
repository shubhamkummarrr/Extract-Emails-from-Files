The city council had been planning the redevelopment of the old transport terminal for nearly two years. During the early discussions, many vendors reached out informally to understand the scope of the project. One contractor mentioned that if additional structural drawings were required, they could simply write to planning.team@metroinfra.org and request the updated files.

Meanwhile the engineering team was busy reviewing several older documents. At one point the assistant engineer scribbled a phone number in the margin — 9897654321 — saying that it belonged to a consultant who had worked on a similar bridge project earlier.

Weeks later, during a late evening meeting, someone mentioned that the land records office might still have the original layout files. They suggested calling the archive department directly on 011-23456789 because the digital records were incomplete.

The conversation then moved on to traffic diversion plans and the timeline for demolition of the old structures. Several participants continued debating whether temporary pedestrian bridges would be required during construction.
