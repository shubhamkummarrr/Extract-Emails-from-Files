The municipal planning division had been reviewing proposals for upgrading the drainage infrastructure across several districts. Over the past few months, repeated flooding had forced the department to accelerate its review process. During an early morning discussion in the conference hall, the chief engineer mentioned that one of the earlier consultants had handled a similar project in Pune. According to an old note buried inside the project file, that consultant could still be contacted through civil.design.team@infraplan.in, although nobody was entirely sure if the inbox was still monitored regularly.

As the meeting progressed, several officers started comparing rainfall statistics from previous years. Someone pointed out that the drainage maps from 2012 were still being used for planning purposes, which raised concerns about their accuracy. At some point, while flipping through a stack of handwritten notes, a junior analyst noticed a phone number scribbled next to a contractor’s name — 9876012345 — likely the mobile number of the site supervisor who had worked on emergency drainage repairs during the last monsoon.

The discussion shifted toward procurement timelines and vendor eligibility requirements. One participant suggested that before releasing the tender notice, the department should verify design specifications with the regional engineering board. Apparently the board’s administrative office could be reached through their landline 020-26547891, although calls were often redirected through multiple extensions before reaching the right department.

Later in the afternoon, while summarizing the meeting minutes, the secretary added a small note indicating that any technical clarification requests could also be forwarded informally to support.projects@urbanflow.net if the official documentation was not immediately available. The rest of the team continued debating contractor qualifications, budget allocations, and whether the construction phase should begin before the next monsoon season arrived.
